My Tech Journey: Finally Taking DSA Seriously

Posted on June 17, 2025 by Aarya Shinde

Introduction

You know that one thing every experienced developer keeps telling you to learn, and you keep nodding like “Yeah, yeah, I’ll get to it” — but never actually do? For me, that was Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA). I had danced around it for years, but this time, I decided to stop procrastinating and really commit. Spoiler: It’s not as scary as I thought… but it’s also not as easy as I hoped.

The Decision

My wake-up call came during a coding interview prep session. I realized I could build projects and solve real-world problems, but when asked to implement a “binary search” from scratch, I suddenly became an ancient Greek philosopher — answering questions with more questions. That’s when I knew: time to face the DSA beast head-on.

The First Few Weeks

I started with arrays and linked lists, thinking, “How hard can this be?” Turns out, harder than I expected. I had to rewire my brain to think in terms of memory, complexity, and efficiency — not just “will it work?”.

My early practice sessions were a mix of small wins (finally understanding how a stack works) and humbling defeats (trying to implement quicksort without crying). The important thing? I kept showing up, problem after problem.

Breakthrough Moments

One magical moment was solving my first graph traversal problem. I still remember the “aha!” feeling when my BFS code finally returned the correct output — and didn’t take a million years to run.

The more I practiced, the more patterns I started to see. Problems that once felt like puzzles from another dimension started to look… familiar.

Lessons from DSA

  • Consistency beats intensity: Solving 2–3 problems every day adds up fast.
  • Understand, don’t memorize: If you can explain a concept without looking at code, you’ve truly learned it.
  • Track your progress: Watching your “solved” count go up is surprisingly motivating.
  • Take breaks: Brute-forcing your brain rarely works; sometimes the solution hits you in the shower.

What’s Next?

My plan is to tackle more advanced topics — dynamic programming, heaps, and maybe even segment trees (which currently sound like something from a fantasy novel). I also want to mix DSA with real-world projects so it doesn’t feel like I’m solving problems in a vacuum.

Conclusion

Taking DSA seriously has been humbling, frustrating, and incredibly rewarding. If you’ve been avoiding it like I did, here’s your sign to just start. The first few steps will feel slow, but one day you’ll look back and realize you’re solving problems you once thought were impossible. And that’s a pretty great feeling.
