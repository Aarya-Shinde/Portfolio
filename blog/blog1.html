My Tech Journey: Learning Python

Posted on June 17, 2025 by Aarya Shinde

Introduction

So, picture this: me, a cup of coffee, a slightly overconfident Google search history, and a dream to learn Python. This is the story of how I went from “Wait, computers can do that?” to “Oh wow… I just automated my own chores.” If you’ve ever been curious about coding — or just want to see someone else’s trial-and-error journey — pull up a chair. This one’s for you.

Early Curiosity

I’ve always had this itch to figure out how things work. As a kid, I’d open up random gadgets around the house (sorry, Mom) and poke around inside like some kind of DIY surgeon. Spoiler alert: my “patients” rarely survived. But the thrill of discovery stuck with me.

First Steps in Python

My Python story started when I stumbled across a random YouTube video titled “Learn Python in 10 Minutes”. Ten minutes later, I had typed out my first print("Hello, World!") and felt like I’d just cracked the Da Vinci Code.

Of course, reality hit when I tried my first “real” program — which broke spectacularly. But there was something about Python’s clean, almost human-like syntax that made me think: “I can actually do this.” And so, late nights, endless tutorials, and an unhealthy number of Stack Overflow visits became my new normal.

Small Wins, Big Motivation

My first proud moment? Automating renaming hundreds of messy image files in one go. It saved me hours and made me feel like a wizard. After that, I built tiny games, scraped websites, and even made a basic chatbot that only sometimes misunderstood me. Progress, right?

Lessons Learned

  • Break it down: Big problems are just lots of little problems stacked together. Python makes it easy to chip away at them one step at a time.
  • Read error messages: They’re not insults from your computer — they’re hints. Sometimes snarky hints, but still hints.
  • Google is your best friend: Seriously, half of programming is searching “Why does my Python code hate me?”
  • Celebrate small wins: Printing “Hello, World!” was as much of a win for me as finishing my first full project.

What’s Next?

Python opened the door to so many areas I want to explore next — data analysis, machine learning, maybe even building a web app that people outside my immediate family will use. I’ll keep sharing what I learn here, including my inevitable mistakes (so you can avoid them… or at least laugh with me).

Conclusion

Learning Python wasn’t just about picking up a programming language — it was about learning how to think differently. And if you’re on the fence about starting, trust me: dive in. The best time to start was yesterday, but the second best time is right now. See you in the next post — hopefully with fewer bugs in my code.
